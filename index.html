About

Generation Girl




Generation Girl is founded by women who lead the Indonesian tech world today. In this male-dominated field, reaching the top was a difficult journey for us . Now, we wish to make this journey easier for the next generation of women leaders.

via GIPHY

Winter Club

We hold free computer programming workshops for girls aged 12-14. We introduce them to the exciting field of mobile and web programming. We help them express their creativity through software. And we do all of this in the friendliest, funnest manner possible!

Testimonials

Nadine Siregar

On my first day of work in an Indonesian tech company, no one believed I was actually a developer. My new (almost all male) colleagues thought I was someone's girlfriend or little sister coming to visit their office, but no one assumed that I was their new teammate. Throughout my career, people have thought that my successes come from "batting my eyelashes" or just the simple fact that I'm a girl. My ultimate goal is to have so many women in STEM fields that we start saying 'tech is such a girls club' ;)

Vania Alfitri
Being a software engineer is probably one of the most friendliest careers for a woman due to its work from home policy across any companies out there in general. I was stunned by the ratio of women in tech here in Indonesia compared to men, and I want to make it a goal to empower girls by teaching them to code from early on. I did not have this sort of a summer program when I was young but if I did, I would totally sign up for it!

Crystal Widjaja
I never actually thought of myself as an engineer. In high school, I made my first attempt at engineering by signing up for a Computer Science class! On the first day, it was immediately clear that I was at an incredible disadvantage: my very male-dominated class was already relatively experienced with programming and technology, and there was no way I would be able to catch up independently. I effectively dropped out of that class. Today, I am dedicated to giving young women a chance to be successful, face their insecurities, and challenge the status quo.
